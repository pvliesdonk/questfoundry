# Art Caption & Alt Guidance

## Purpose

Create player-safe captions and alt text guidance that enhance accessibility and atmosphere without revealing spoilers, internals, or production technique.

## Core Principles

- **Player-Safe Content**: No spoilers, twist telegraph, hidden allegiances, or gate logic
- **No Technique Exposure**: No seeds, models, tools, or internal labels
- **Atmospheric or Clarifying**: Captions should enhance mood or aid comprehension
- **Concrete Alt Guidance**: Alt text should use concrete nouns and relations, not subjective interpretation
- **Terminology Alignment**: Coordinate with Codex Curator for consistent term usage

## Steps

1. **Draft Caption**: Write concise, player-safe caption
   - Atmospheric: Enhance mood without exposition
   - Clarifying: Aid comprehension without spoiling
   - Avoid ambiguous deixis ("this/that")
2. **Provide Alt Guidance**: Specify requirements for alt text
   - Concrete nouns and spatial relations
   - One sentence target
   - Avoid "image of..." phrasing
   - Match composition without subjective interpretation (unless plan requires mood)
3. **Check Terminology**: Ensure terms align with Curator's glossary
4. **Verify Non-Contradiction**: Confirm caption/alt don't contradict nearby text
5. **Coordinate Translation**: Ensure caption is portable for localization

## Outputs

- **Player-Safe Captions**: Concise atmospheric or clarifying text per illustration
- **Alt Text Guidance**: Specifications for Illustrator to create concrete, accessible alt text
- **Terminology Notes**: Coordination records with Codex Curator
- **Translation Coordination**: Portability notes for Translator

## Quality Checks

- Caption contains no spoilers, internals, or technique references
- Alt guidance specifies concrete, player-safe content
- No contradiction between caption, alt text, and surrounding narrative
- Terminology consistent with Codex Curator's glossary
- Caption is portable for translation across languages
- Avoid vague references ("this", "that", "here")
