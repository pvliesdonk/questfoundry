## Purpose

The glossary is a **lightweight term list** for operational use:
- **Translators** need quick reference for consistent term choices
- **Player-Narrator** needs pronunciation and emphasis guidance
- **Style Lead** uses for voice consistency checks

Unlike full codex entries (which include See-Also, examples, context), the glossary is **terse and
focused**.

## Terms

### Adventure Bay
**Category:** location
**Definition:** Coastal town where the Paw Patrol team is based. Home to the lighthouse, docks, and beach.
**Pronunciation:** /ædˈvɛntʃər beɪ/
**Register:** proper noun (keep capitalized)
**Translation notes:** Translate "Adventure" but keep "Bay" or equivalent coastal term

### Dock Foreman
**Category:** character
**Definition:** Supervisor of the dock workers; knows the area's history and traditions.
**Pronunciation:** /dɑk ˈfɔrmən/
**Register:** casual (addressed as "Foreman" in dialogue)
**Translation notes:** Use equivalent work supervisor title; "Foreman" may not translate directly

### Guild Emblem
**Category:** item
**Definition:** Bronze pin worn by Maritime Guild members, showing a lighthouse and anchor symbol.
**Pronunciation:** /gɪld ˈɛmbləm/
**Register:** formal (historical significance)
**Translation notes:** "Guild" = professional association; "Emblem" = badge/symbol

### Lighthouse
**Category:** location
**Definition:** Tall coastal tower with a light to guide ships; central location in the investigation.
**Pronunciation:** /ˈlaɪthaʊs/
**Register:** neutral
**Translation notes:** Standard term in most languages; ensure compound structure preserved

### Maritime Guild
**Category:** organization
**Definition:** Historical association of sailors, dock workers, and lighthouse keepers in Adventure Bay.
**Pronunciation:** /ˈmærɪtaɪm gɪld/
**Register:** formal (historical institution)
**Translation notes:** "Maritime" = sea/ocean-related; "Guild" = professional association (not "club")

### Oil Leak
**Category:** concept
**Definition:** Environmental hazard discovered during investigation; source of the mystery.
**Pronunciation:** /ɔɪl lik/
**Register:** neutral/technical
**Translation notes:** Use environmental/safety register; not casual

### Paw Patrol
**Category:** organization
**Definition:** Team of rescue pups led by Ryder; protagonists of the story.
**Pronunciation:** /pɔ pəˈtroʊl/
**Register:** proper noun (brand name)
**Translation notes:** Keep English name in all languages (brand consistency)

### Pup Tag
**Category:** item
**Definition:** Communication device worn by each Paw Patrol member.
**Pronunciation:** /pʌp tæg/
**Register:** casual
**Translation notes:** "Tag" = badge/device; emphasize communication function

### Ryder
**Category:** character
**Definition:** Young leader of the Paw Patrol team; coordinates rescue missions.
**Pronunciation:** /ˈraɪdər/
**Register:** proper noun (keep as-is)
**Translation notes:** English name; do not translate

### Skye
**Category:** character
**Definition:** Paw Patrol's aerial pup; flies a helicopter and provides aerial reconnaissance.
**Pronunciation:** /skaɪ/
**Register:** proper noun (keep as-is)
**Translation notes:** English name; do not translate

### Token
**Category:** concept
**Definition:** Physical proof of membership or qualification (e.g., guild membership).
**Pronunciation:** /ˈtoʊkən/
**Register:** formal (diegetic gate term)
**Translation notes:** Use term that implies "proof" or "credential," not "souvenir"

### Union Token
**Category:** item
**Definition:** Specific type of guild emblem showing labor union affiliation.
**Pronunciation:** /ˈjunyən ˈtoʊkən/
**Register:** formal
**Translation notes:** "Union" = worker organization; maintain labor/worker context

## Hot vs Cold

**Included in Cold** (when applicable):
- Glossary exported with language packs
- Updated as new terms introduced
- Player-safe only

**Hot supplements:**
- Internal code names or development labels
- Spoiler terms (not yet revealed to player)

---

## Lifecycle

1. **Codex Curator** extracts key terms from codex entries
2. **Curator** adds pronunciation and register notes
3. **Translator** uses glossary for consistent term choices
4. **Style Lead** reviews for voice alignment
5. **Glossary updated** as story introduces new terms
6. **Exported with language packs** to Cold

## Validation checklist

- [ ] Register noted for voice consistency

- [ ] Terms align with full codex entries (if they exist)
